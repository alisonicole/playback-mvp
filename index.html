The science

Looking back isn't sentimental.
It's how you move forward.

Research shows nostalgia is one of the most underused tools for growth, wellbeing, and self-understanding.

↑
Nostalgia raises self-esteem, optimism, and sense of meaning — consistently across studies.
92%
of people underestimate how much they've changed in the past year.
∞
The "arrival fallacy" — we adapt so fast to growth, we forget we ever wanted it.
→
Reflection on the past motivates better choices going forward. Not comfort — momentum.
"Nostalgia is ultimately a future-oriented experience. It involves reflecting on the past, but it motivates the affective states and goals that improve people's future lives."
University of Southampton · Journal of Frontiers in Psychology
